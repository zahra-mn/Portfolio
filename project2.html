Overview

Analysis, Design, and Implementation of a Management Dashboard for a Road Construction Supervision Company

Project Summary

I worked as a Product Designer in Raha Consulting to lead an extremely sluggish, manual reporting method into a more efficient centralized, real-time executive dashboard. This was a collaboration, where Raha Consulting was working in conjunction with a large road construction supervision firm across the nation. The overall project aimed to resolve delays with executive decisions when they would try to reconcile what were critical project KPI's for their construction projects and assess if they fit into efficiencies and whether they were on track or ahead of budget.

I did not design the actual UI, but I did sketch the initial wireframes, and worked closely with the UI designer to ensure the interface aligned with user experience and business priorities.

Understanding

Key Challenges

There was a manual and distributed reporting, in the form of emails, WhatsApp, and Excel spreadsheets, which introduced delays of 3-5 days, and often produced incomplete or outdated reports. Because the reports were not systematically compiled into an overview, the CEO was unable to get a timely, centralized insight regarding performance, especially in discussions with government stakeholders.

Define

Identifying & Prioritizing Needs + KPIs

I conducted interviews with the CEO, project managers, and technical leads and audited some past reports to figure out which KPIs were going to be the most impactful. Working with the business analyst and product manager, we created a matrix of KPIs, ordered them based on how often we planned to gather the data, score in terms of importance, and whether we had the data available or not. This matrix guided us to a focus on the metrics that would deliver the most impact to the organization.

Reflection

This project reinforced a number of insights:

  • Executive-facing dashboards must be simple, fast and trusted.
  • Wireframing is not simply for design work, it is also part of strategic planning.
  • Designers, business intelligence analysts and developers must work closely to ensure the final product accommodates the expectations of a real-world situation.
  • Designing for stakeholders who operate under extreme pressure (like a CEO) requires empathy and simplicity at the core of the experience.
