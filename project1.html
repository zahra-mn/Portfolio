Project Summary

The project was part of a five-year digital transformation strategy in the Building Engineering Organization, the official organization responsible for construction in that province. The overall program provided digital web platform, dashboards, and various automation of processes and processes to internally streamline operations.

Our focus was a mobile solution in the form of field supervision reports and to relieve manual processes, wireless data inputs, and delays in reporting. This module would help enable real-time reporting no matter where the project was, covering not only accurate reporting from job sites, but also verification of safety and regulations.

Our Mission

To digitize and streamline the on-site reporting of violations and technical issues, enabling real-time, location-based reporting by supervisors — regardless of time or place constraints.

Core Project Recognized

We identified a core problem in the needs assessment stage; legal obligations require supervisors to perform physical site inspections. Yet there were a few challenges related to physical inspections:

1. Possibility of false reporting without someone actually visiting the site.

2. Duplication of reporting (first on a paper version and then in the system) was wasted time.

3. There was no transparency for the owner of a building that couldn't be able to verify if site visits occurred.

Project Objectives

To design a mobile-first, location-based reporting system that would allow building supervisors to easily prepare their comprehensive, verified reports from the construction site. The objectives were:

1. To create a mobile application with a high-quality user experience.

2. To increase the speed and accuracy of field reports.

3. To discourage false reporting without attending the site.

4. To increase confidence in the oversight and quality assurance process for building owners.

Key Findings

Our research uncovered several critical insights that highlight the need for a more efficient, compliant, and user-friendly solution

• 70% of supervisors expressed frustration with having to log into the website repeatedly to upload/permanent documents.

• Users expressed the desire for a quicker, easier and more dependable reporting procedure.

• 40% of reports did not have an actual site visit.

• Several users, as well as stakeholders, expressed concern over safety and compliance, requiring stronger oversight.

Problem Statement

" Field engineers and supervisors require an improved, effective and reliable way to submit construction site reports. Current manual reports can be inefficient, burdensome, repetitive and prone to mistakes which could lead to non-compliance and trust in safety processes. A digital solution is needed to collect and report data accurately and in a timely fashion without taxing the user with a complicated reporting experience for already busy on-site professionals."

Ideation & Brainstorming

We conducted some brainstorming sessions with the stakeholders of the project and the UX team based on the user interviews, workflow analysis, and pain points. During these sessions, we generated ideas that addressed the three main areas of accuracy, trust, and ease-of-use, using "How Might We" questions to get the ideas flowing. We did some journey mapping with quick sketches, prioritizing must-have features such as real-time site verification, testing ideas for their user needs, and regulatory needs.

Proposed Solution

A smart mobile app that ensures supervisors can submit reports only when they are physically present on-site, verified through GPS and signal checks. To make the process secure and convenient, the app includes:

Smart real-time location verification
Geo-fencing to restrict submissions within the approved site radius
Structured forms for easy and accurate inspection data entry
Photo and document uploads with secure timestamps for added credibility
OTP-based login for quick and secure access
Digital signatures for automated report authentication
Draft saving so users can complete reports in multiple sessions
Overdue notifications to remind users of pending submissions
Project overview dashboard for supervisors to see all upcoming and past reports at a glance

Testing & Validation

Method

2-week pilot test with 8 real supervisors on actual projects

Outcomes

Tracking reports submission time reduced from an average of 25 minutes to 8 minutes

Over 80% satisfaction from users of the app on their experience

All parties associated with buildings provided positive feedback on transparency and trust

Lessons Learned

Continuous engagement with end users and stakeholders is crucial to addressing complex and organizational issues.

Data integrity is key to oversight processes; technology must help ensure data integrity.

Designing for busy, low-tech users (e.g. engineers), requires a pared back design and circular testing approach.

Overall, this was a successful case of integrating system analysis, UX/UI design, and technology development to solve important real-world problems. I am proud to have contributed to the redesign of an important process in the national construction supervisor model.
